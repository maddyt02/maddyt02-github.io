Things I Enjoy

One of the most memorable trips I have taken recently was to Guatemala with my roommate Lorelei. The trip began in Antigua, a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its historic architecture and beautiful surroundings. While there, we explored many historic buildings and churches and took a tour of a local coffee farm, where we learned about the process of growing and producing Guatemalan coffee. Antigua is an incredibly charming town filled with colorful streets, markets, and shops, and it is surrounded by stunning volcano views. Visiting local markets and restaurants also gave us the opportunity to learn more about Guatemalan cuisine and products.

During our stay in Antigua, we completed an overnight hike up the Acatenango volcano. The hike was physically very challenging, but it was also one of the most rewarding experiences of the trip. After reaching base camp at Acatanengo, we hiked Fuego, a different active volcano, and we got to see its eruptions up close. Along the hike we met travelers from Germany, Canada, Mexico, and the United States, and our group quickly became friends. We spent the rest of the trip exploring together and sharing experiences.

After Antigua, we traveled to Lake Atitlán and stayed in the town of San Pedro. There we completed the Indian Nose sunrise hike and explored nearby towns like San Marcos and San Juan. One of my favorite parts of the trip was practicing Spanish and connecting with locals. Using Spanish helped us meet people while out at restaurants and bars, and at one point my friends and I were even invited to share a meal in a local shop owner’s home.
